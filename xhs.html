Comparative Usability Study of XiaoHongShu vs TikTok

Jan - Apr 2025

Social Media Platforms as E-Commerce Engines: A Comparative Study of Xiaohongshu, TikTok, and Their Role in Consumer Purchasing Decisions in China, South Korea, and the United States.

Introduction: Mobile Phones as Human Extensions

In today’s world, it’s nearly impossible to find someone without a mobile phone — and for most, it’s a constant companion. Phones have become extensions of the human body, enabling us to text, learn, entertain, and connect from virtually anywhere. Unlike computers, mobile phones are truly “mobile,” allowing people to stay productive and engaged on the go.

This accessibility has transformed industries, especially e-commerce. Consumers now browse and buy products from anywhere: at home, on public transit, even while traveling abroad. As social media platforms integrate e-commerce features, a new hybrid space has emerged: one where digital interaction and commerce converge.

Why This Matters

  • Cultural Relevance: Platforms like Xiaohongshu (Little Red Book) and TikTok are reshaping how people shop, socialize, and express identity online.
  • Global Impact: These apps are among the highest-grossing globally, influencing consumer behavior across borders.
  • Design Implications: Understanding how users engage with these platforms can inform more inclusive, culturally attuned UX strategies.

Research Focus

This study explores how Xiaohongshu and TikTok influence purchasing decisions across two regions:

  • China
  • United States

By comparing platform features, e-commerce models, and cultural contexts, the research identifies key factors that shape consumer behavior and engagement.

Platform Overviews

Xiaohongshu (Little Red Book)

Xiaohongshu is a leading Chinese social e-commerce platform that blends lifestyle inspiration with online shopping. It’s known for its intimate, review-driven community and focus on beauty, fashion, and luxury goods.

  • Founded: 2013 in Shanghai
  • 400+ million registered users as of 2023
  • ~90% of active users are women, ages 18–34
  • Users access the app 6–7 times daily on average
  • Backed by Alibaba, Tencent, GGV Capital

TikTok

TikTok is a global short-form video platform that merges entertainment, creativity, and commerce. Its rapid growth and influencer-driven culture make it a powerful force in shaping consumer behavior.

  • Launched internationally in 2017 by ByteDance
  • Over 2 billion global users
  • 136.4 million users in the U.S. alone
  • Americans spend $32 million per day on TikTok Shop

Research Focus & Methodology

Research Question

How do Xiaohongshu and TikTok influence consumer purchasing decisions in China and the United States, and what are the key cultural and platform-specific factors that drive these differences?

Why These Countries?

  • China: Home to Xiaohongshu’s primary user base and a global leader in social commerce.
  • United States: TikTok’s largest market, with deep integration of e-commerce features.

Methodology

  • Literature Review: Research on social media, e-commerce, and consumer behavior.
  • UX Comparative Analysis: Observation of platform features, UI, and e-commerce integration.
  • Qualitative Insights: Observer accounts to explore how platforms drive purchasing behavior.
  • Quantitative Data Review: Usage stats and consumer trends across China and the U.S.

E-Commerce Integration: How TikTok and Xiaohongshu Turn Browsing into Buying

Social media platforms are no longer just spaces for connection — they’re marketplaces. TikTok and Xiaohongshu have each developed distinct e-commerce ecosystems that blend entertainment, community, and shopping into a single user experience.

Xiaohongshu: Curated Commerce in a Closed Ecosystem

  • Embedded product links in posts make shopping feel organic and user-driven
  • Stores inventory in its own warehouses for quality control
  • Dedicated shopping tools: Order, Cart, Wallet
  • Gamified engagement with Potato Coins

TikTok: Viral Trends Meet Impulse Shopping

  • Shop via product tags or Shop tab
  • Checkout via Venmo, PayPal, or Apple Pay
  • Influencers drive engagement with reviews and tutorials

Influencer Culture & Consumer Behavior

Xiaohongshu: Authenticity, Attachment & Live Streaming

Xiaohongshu’s influencer ecosystem is built on layered content types, creating a peer-driven environment where recommendations feel personal.

TikTok: Entertainment, Trends & Emotional Hooks

TikTok’s influencer strategy centers on storytelling and trend participation. Products are introduced through personal stories, tutorials, and challenges.

Consumer Motivation & Trust

Xiaohongshu: Peer-based influence driven by social proof and emotional relatability.
TikTok: Impulse buying and cultural participation driven by urgency and trends.

Conclusion: What This Means for UX & E-Commerce

  • Xiaohongshu builds trust through peer reviews and curated shopping.
  • TikTok drives impulse buying through trends and entertainment.
  • Design matters: reflect emotional tone and shopping habits.
  • Culture matters: what works in one market may not in another.

Social commerce is changing not just how people shop — but how they feel about shopping.
